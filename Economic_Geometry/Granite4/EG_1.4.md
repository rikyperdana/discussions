#### 1.4 The Method of Writing This Book

This book was majorly—by a large portion—written by AI. But not the ordinary free tier generative AI from popular platforms like ChatGPT, Gemini, or DeepSeek. The platform I've been using was ran locally through Ollama engine in my 2GB Nvidia GTX 1050 powered PC. The model of choice here—among myriad of smart and small LLMs—fell into IBM Granite 4, it's only 2GB which fits neatly in my low-powered GPU. From my personal experience, this model was good enough for learning and knowledge exploration, like having a interactive mini digital encyclopedia.

I said the word majorly because we can't let this model work alone and generate sloppy results due to its generative—or even halucinative—nature. So a good prompting technique and experience is necessary. Throughout the process of writing this book, I had to read what it generates to make sure that the result are both reasonable and engaging. What's the point of reasonable—college book styled—dry contents if it's not engaging? And what's the point of engaging if it failed to be reasonable.

My general strategy of writing this book is start from generating a proper table of contents. 4 to 5 times changing the entire prompts to get satisfactory results is a norm here, we can't expect our little LLM buddy to be correct at first try. And for every formulas, codes, or something slightly complex has to be experimented first manually to prevent publishing something non-sensical—or worse—irresponsible.

As a side project of a hobby—rather than rushing things—I resort to write only a sub-chapter a day. This part is important, because I don't want to let my System 2 to be exhausted as well as prevent my System 1 to be overconfident. A slow but steady stream of quality generated results are more preferable in this writing method.

Different from how we use the mainstream AI platforms where the results are yield in continuous thread, I chose to only involve the TOC for sub-chapter writing instruction. This is intentional because a 2GB model has short context which prevents us from getting the desired results as well as a way of saving the processing cost; electricity ain't cheap these days.

In essence, this book represents a unique blend of AI and human ingenuity—a testament to what can be achieved when we leverage technology while maintaining our critical oversight. It's not just about understanding the intersection of geometry and economics through an algorithmic lens but also appreciating the nuances that only a human perspective can offer. This approach ensures that the reader gains both depth and breadth in their comprehension, setting a new standard for how such interdisciplinary studies can be approached in the digital age.
