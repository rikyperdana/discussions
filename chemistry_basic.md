I want to learn chemistry from basic to intermediat level
Okay, that's a fantastic goal! Chemistry is a foundational science, and a solid understanding will open doors to many other fields. Here's a structured plan to learn chemistry from basic to intermediate levels, broken down into steps, resources, and tips.  I'll also include some recommended resources.

**Phase 1: The Very Basics (Conceptual Foundation - ~2-4 weeks)**

*   **Topics:**
    *   **Matter & Measurement:**  What is matter? States of matter (solid, liquid, gas, plasma).  Mass, volume, density, moles, and molar mass.  Significant figures.
    *   **Atomic Structure:**  Atoms, protons, neutrons, electrons.  Atomic number, mass number, isotopes.  Electron configuration (brief introduction – just the basics of shells and subshells).
    *   **The Periodic Table:**  Organization of the periodic table. Metals, nonmetals, metalloids.  Groups and periods.  Trends in atomic radius, ionization energy, electronegativity.
    *   **Chemical Bonding:**  Ionic, covalent, and metallic bonds.  Lewis structures.  Valence electrons.
    *   **Chemical Reactions:**  Chemical equations, balancing equations.  Reactants and products.  Types of reactions (synthesis, decomposition, single displacement, double displacement, combustion).
*   **Resources:**
    *   **Khan Academy:**  Excellent introductory chemistry videos and practice exercises.  (https://www.khanacademy.org/science/chemistry)  Start with the "Introduction to Chemistry" course.
    *   **Crash Course Chemistry (YouTube):**  A fun and engaging overview of basic concepts. (https://www.youtube.com/playlist?list=PL3EBDD41581443933)
    *   **Textbooks (Optional):**  A very basic introductory chemistry textbook can be helpful.  Look for ones geared towards high school or introductory college courses.  (See "Recommended Textbooks" at the end).
*   **Activities:**
    *   Practice calculating density, molar mass, and converting between units.
    *   Draw Lewis structures for simple molecules (e.g., water, methane, carbon dioxide).
    *   Balance simple chemical equations.
    *   Use online simulations to visualize atomic structure and bonding.

**Phase 2: Building a Strong Foundation (Core Chemistry - ~6-8 weeks)**

*   **Topics:**
    *   **Stoichiometry:**  Mole ratios, limiting reactants, theoretical yield, percent yield.
    *   **States of Matter (Detailed):**  Gases (kinetic molecular theory, ideal gas law), liquids, solids.  Phase changes.
    *   **Solutions:**  Solubility, concentration (molarity, molality, percent concentration).  Colligative properties (boiling point elevation, freezing point depression).
    *   **Acids and Bases:**  pH scale, strong acids and bases, weak acids and bases, buffers.  Acid-base reactions.
    *   **Chemical Kinetics:**  Reaction rates, rate laws, activation energy.
    *   **Thermochemistry:**  Enthalpy, exothermic and endothermic reactions, Hess's Law.
*   **Resources:**
    *   **Khan Academy:** Continue with the "Chemistry" course.
    *   **OpenStax Chemistry:** A free, open-source chemistry textbook. (https://openstax.org/details/books/chemistry)
    *   **Chem LibreTexts:** A comprehensive online chemistry textbook. (https://chem.libretexts.org/)
    *   **MIT OpenCourseWare:**  Look for introductory chemistry courses. (https://ocw.mit.edu/courses/chemistry/)
*   **Activities:**
    *   Solve stoichiometry problems involving mole ratios and limiting reactants.
    *   Calculate molarity and prepare solutions.
    *   Perform calculations related to pH and buffers.
    *   Practice drawing reaction mechanisms.
    *   Work through problems involving enthalpy and Hess's Law.

**Phase 3: Intermediate Chemistry (Deeper Dive - ~8-12 weeks)**

*   **Topics:**
    *   **Organic Chemistry (Introduction):**  Basic organic nomenclature, functional groups (alkanes, alkenes, alkynes, alcohols, ethers, aldehydes, ketones, carboxylic acids).  Isomers.
    *   **Redox Reactions:**  Oxidation-reduction principles, half-reactions, balancing redox equations.
    *   **Coordination Chemistry:**  Ligands, coordination complexes, crystal field theory (basic).
    *   **Electrochemistry:**  Electrochemical cells, galvanic cells, electrolytic cells, corrosion.
    *   **Quantum Chemistry (Introduction):**  Wave-particle duality, Schrödinger equation (conceptual).
*   **Resources:**
    *   **OpenStax Chemistry:** (Continue using it)
    *   **Chem LibreTexts:** (Continue using it)
    *   **Organic Chemistry Tutor (YouTube):**  Excellent for organic chemistry explanations. (https://www.youtube.com/@OrganicChemistryTutor)
    *   **MIT OpenCourseWare:**  Look for intermediate chemistry courses.
    *   **University Lecture Notes:** Many universities post lecture notes and problem sets online. Search for courses on topics like "Organic Chemistry I" or "Inorganic Chemistry I."
*   **Activities:**
    *   Name organic compounds using IUPAC nomenclature.
    *   Draw structures of isomers.
    *   Balance redox equations.
    *   Write half-reactions and calculate cell potentials.
    *   Work through problems involving coordination complexes.
    *   Explore basic quantum chemical concepts.

**Tools & Tips for Success:**

*   **Practice, Practice, Practice:** Chemistry is a problem-solving subject.  Work through as many problems as possible.
*   **Understand the Concepts:** Don't just memorize formulas.  Strive to understand *why* things work the way they do.
*   **Draw Diagrams:**  Visualizing concepts (e.g., Lewis structures, reaction mechanisms) can be very helpful.
*   **Use Online Simulations:**  Simulations can help you visualize abstract concepts.
*   **Form a Study Group:**  Discussing concepts with others can deepen your understanding.
*   **Don't Be Afraid to Ask for Help:**  If you're stuck, ask a teacher, tutor, or online forum for assistance.
*   **Consistency is Key:**  Set aside regular time for studying, even if it's just for 30 minutes each day.
*   **Use a good calculator:** A scientific calculator is essential for chemistry.
*   **Stay Organized:** Keep your notes and materials organized.
